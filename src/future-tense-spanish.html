What is the Future Tense in Spanish?

The future tense in Spanish is used to talk about actions that will happen in the future. Unlike English, Spanish forms the future tense by adding specific endings to the infinitive form of the verb.

Examples:

  • Hablar (to speak) → Yo hablaré (I will speak)
  • Comer (to eat) → Nosotros comeremos (We will eat)
  • Vivir (to live) → Ellos vivirán (They will live)

How to Form the Future Tense in Spanish?

The future tense is simple to conjugate! Just take the infinitive verb and add these endings:

Subject Ending Example (Hablar)
Yo -é Hablaré
Tú -ás Hablarás
Él/Ella/Usted -á Hablará
Nosotros/as -emos Hablaremos
Vosotros/as -éis Hablaréis
Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes -án Hablarán

Irregular Verbs in the Future Tense

Some verbs don’t follow the regular pattern and have irregular stems. However, they still use the same endings.

Infinitive Stem Change Example
Tener (to have) Tendr- Tendré (I will have)
Hacer (to do/make) Har- Haré (I will do)
Decir (to say) Dir- Diré (I will say)
Poder (to be able) Podr- Podré (I will be able)

When to Use the Future Tense?

The Spanish future tense is versatile and can be used in various contexts, such as:

Predictions and Forecasts

  • Mañana nevará en Madrid. (Tomorrow, it will snow in Madrid.)
  • Los científicos descubrirán nuevas formas de vida en el océano. (Scientists will discover new life forms in the ocean.)

Hypothetical or Uncertain Situations

  • ¿Quién ganará el partido esta noche? (Who will win the game tonight?)
  • Probablemente llegaré tarde. (I will probably arrive late.)

Promises and Commitments

  • Te ayudaré con tu tarea. (I will help you with your homework.)
  • No lo volveré a hacer. (I will not do it again.)

Orders and Instructions

  • Llamarás a tu madre todos los domingos. (You will call your mother every Sunday.)
  • Me entregarás el informe mañana. (You will deliver the report to me tomorrow.)

Future Tense vs. Other Ways to Talk About the Future

Spanish has multiple ways to express future actions, and the future tense is just one of them! Compare these examples:

  • Future Tense: Mañana estudiaré español. (Tomorrow I will study Spanish.)
  • Ir + a + Infinitive (Near Future): Voy a estudiar español mañana. (I am going to study Spanish tomorrow.)
  • Present Tense for Future Meaning: Mañana tengo un examen. (Tomorrow I have an exam.)

More Uses of the Future Tense

  • Making Assumptions
    • Serán las cinco de la tarde. (It must be around five in the afternoon.)
    • Estará cansado después de trabajar tanto. (He must be tired after working so much.)
  • Expressing Probability
    • Probablemente llegaré a las ocho. (I will probably arrive at eight.)
    • Tal vez lloverá esta noche. (Maybe it will rain tonight.)
  • Expressing Determination
    • No te preocuparás por nada, yo me encargaré. (You will not worry about anything, I will take care of it.)
    • Conseguiré ese trabajo pase lo que pase. (I will get that job no matter what.)
